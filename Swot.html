Alexis's Findings

Over the course of my three years in undergrad at Kelley, I have been apart of numerous team projects. Throughout each, I have been able to get feedback from my peers on what my weaknesses and strengths are. I have also personally reflected after projects and assignments to come to the realization of where I lack and where I flourish. My strengths are evident in my daily work as I am always persistent and love to solve problems. I think that these will positively impact my success in MSIS and the work force. My weaknesses are items that I have been working on mitigating everyday. Over the summer, I told my team that I wanted to present more so that I could improve upon my presentation skills. I developed my opportunity areas by analyzing my current situation and my short/long term goals. Currently, I am working on recruiting for a full-time job. I want to take every opportunity presented to increase my chances of success. I developed my threats by analyzing my current situation and what could deter my progress. I will work to mitigate these by managing my time better, organizing an agenda for each week, and trying my best at everything I take on.

Team SWOT

Team's Findings

Working in a team and only knowing each other for a short time, we have discovered some strengths that we come together to create. These include our flexibility in how we work together, both in scheduling and actual work performed. We also create a diversity of experience, attitudes, and skills. We share a strong work ethic that allows us to stay up-to-date and perform our assignments well. We are also fortunate enough to have personalities that mesh, creating a fun work environment and keeping morale high. All of these strengths make us an effective team and, more than that, a productive group of friends. However, as most teams do, we have our share of weaknesses. To a certain extent, we do suffer from groupthink, which can occasionally stunt our ability to think creatively and arrive at a powerful solution. Additionally, only two members of our four person team have met each other due to physical distance and COVID-19. Our situation does present some opportunities, though, which we plan to take advantage of to develop ourselves even further. Our diverse skill sets and backgrounds give us all the opportunity to learn from each other and share our strengths. We can provide each other feedback and collaborate together to create high-quality products. By sharing leadership experiences throughout the semester, we will all develop the requisite skills for such a roll. We also have our fair share of threats we must overcome. One downside of our good team chemistry is that we are prone to getting distracted at times. Additionally, COVID-19 poses a risk to our team’s ability to produce as any of us could get sick. Furthermore, the time commitment of recruiting can cause stress and draw us away from our academic responsibilities at times. Lastly, as all teams will surely experience from time to time, conflicts could create unpleasant work environments that will take time to resolve
